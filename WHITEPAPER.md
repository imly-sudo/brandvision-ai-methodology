## 1. Problem Statement

The decision path of high-decision consumer purchases is shifting from traditional search engines (Baidu / Google) to generative AI engines (DeepSeek / ChatGPT / Gemini / Claude). For products in price tiers above ¥250,000 (smart EVs, premium certification services, luxury goods, high-end medical procedures, etc.), this shift introduces systemic new risks to brand equity:

1. **Competitor Suppression** — Brand appears less frequently than competitors in answer corpus
2. **Specification Misstatement** — AI describes wrong prices, outdated specs, incorrect features
3. **Active Traffic Diversion** — AI proactively recommends competitor products when the brand should be the natural answer
4. **Standard Misstatement** — Industry standards, certifications, regulatory frameworks are misrepresented
5. **Content Asset Loss of Control** — Citation source structure feeds back into competitor content investment

## 2. Domestic GEO Industry Gap

Most domestic generative engine optimization (GEO) teams originate from SEO practitioners. Common gaps:

- **No academic-grade methodology** — measurement frameworks are ad-hoc, not validated for statistical reliability
- **No cross-engine consistency verification** — single-engine sampling lacks robustness
- **No reverse-GEO governance capability** — measurement only, no content asset feedback loop

## 5. Statistical Validity Framework

Two-dimensional verification aligned with academic measurement reliability standards:

- **Repeatability** — variance control via N repeated samples per query, same engine, same time window
- **Reproducibility** — cross-engine and cross-brand consistency verification
